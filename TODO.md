# Known gaps and open items

State as of 2026-07-20, course v1.0 — 14 modules, 129 sections, 174 figures,
311 exercises, ~191,000 words, 51 references.

Written down so it survives a new session. Nothing here is broken; these are
things a future pass should know about. Ordered by how much they matter.

---

## Content gaps

### 1. Interoception is entirely absent

Zero mentions across all 129 sections. The visceral senses — cardiac,
respiratory, gastrointestinal, osmotic — are a genuine sensory modality with a
normative story (predictive regulation / allostasis) and they are missing.

**Why it might matter here.** Interoception's consumer is a *regulator* rather
than a percept, which makes it a second instance of the §7 argument that
perception can be a by-product of machinery built for control. It would also
give the course a modality whose "stimulus" is generated by the body itself,
which nothing else covers. There is a chemosensory angle too — gut nutrient
sensing uses receptor families overlapping with taste.

**Why it is not urgent.** It does not bear on the central thesis the way the
vomeronasal system did. It would be an eighth modality module, not a fix.

### 2. Nociception is thin

Two passing mentions (`05-gustation/04-receptor-array.md`,
`08-exotic/04-infrared.md`, the latter about a nociceptive channel repurposed
for infrared). Pain is treated as part of somatosensation and never given its
own treatment.

**The missed argument:** nociception has a *grossly asymmetric loss function* —
a missed injury costs far more than a false alarm — which is structurally the
same argument §5 makes about bitter taste. The course makes that argument once
and could make it twice, in a modality where the asymmetry is even starker. A
section in §4 would probably suffice; it does not need a module.

### 3. Thermoreception

One mention. Partly covered obliquely by the infrared section in §8. Lowest
priority of the three — the physics is covered, the normative angle is thin.

## Method caveats already stated in the course, recorded here so they are not
## quietly forgotten

- **The datasheet scores are hand-assigned ordinals.** `datasheet.js` and
  `modality-space.js` both run on 0–10 scores authored to summarise the prose.
  The correlations they display (notably ensemble-vs-theory, r = 0.91) are
  therefore partly a measure of authoring consistency, not only of the world.
  Both captions say so. Do not let anyone quote r = 0.91 as a result.

- **The overclaiming scan was targeted, not exhaustive.** It covered modules
  4–7 and checked known-controversial claims rather than reading all 129
  sections line by line. It found two real problems (Clarke's nucleus stated
  without citation and without the biomechanical caveat; MSTd cue combination
  stated as exact optimality). **Modules 9 (Development and Evolution) and 12
  (Artificial Sensing) are the newest and have never had such a scan.**

- **§12 fails its own criterion one, deliberately and in writing.** The
  artificial-sensing argument rests on three engineered domains chosen by the
  author with the conclusion already known. §12.6 says this. It is the same
  failure the module attributes to olfactory theory.

## Test-harness note (this bites on re-run)

Several headless suites in the scratchpad use **fixed** `waitForTimeout` values
that were calibrated when the course was 87 sections. At 129 sections and 311
exercises they are too short, and they fail in a way that *looks* like a real
defect:

- The full-course render sweep reported figures as undrawn because counts
  leaked from the previous section before it had re-rendered.
- The transfer suite reported "import silently overwrites" — which would be a
  data-loss bug — when in fact the import diff simply took longer than 700 ms
  to render, because it now reconciles against 129 sections.

`transfer-test.mjs` and the render sweep have been converted to wait on
conditions rather than the clock. **If a suite fails after adding content,
suspect the harness before the site**, and verify the specific page or flow
individually before believing it.

## Structural notes

- **The eleven-section skeleton applies to §1–§7 only.** §8 onward have their
  own structures because they ask different questions — a module on convergent
  evolution has no "receptor array" section to fill. §0.0 explains this.
- **There is no module gap any more.** Exotic Senses moved 9 → 8 and the
  capstone 12 → 13 to make room for Development and Evolution (9) and
  Artificial Sensing (12). Numbering runs 0–13 with no holes.
- **Capstone imports are numbered "Import 1–28", continuous.** They are
  deliberately *not* written as §13.x, because §13.x refers to subsections and
  the two collided in an earlier draft.
- **Only two callout classes have CSS**: `is-key` and `is-invertebrate`. A
  callout with any other class renders unstyled. Verified: content currently
  uses only those two.
- **Exercise ids are the progress store's primary keys.** Renumbering a module
  orphans saved answers in it. Harmless (the store ignores orphans) but not
  free — do renumbering early if at all.
