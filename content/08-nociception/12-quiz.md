<x-callout class="x-callout is-key">
<div class="x-callout-title">What to carry into the next module</div>
Nociception contributes one correction and three tools.

<strong>The correction: adaptation has a sign, and it is predictable</strong> (§8.10). Adjust gain to maximise the report's decision value, given how long the reported variable stays actionable. Reduces to the old principle for transient referents; reverses for persistent ones. Every earlier module's §10 should be reread with the sign made explicit.

<strong>A receptive field can be defined by a consequence</strong> (§8.1). No physical quantity, no units, no ensemble — and polymodal transduction with OR combination as the architectural signature. This is the existence proof the capstone needs for consequence-defined olfactory coordinates.

<strong>The percept is not the afferent signal</strong> (§8.7). Both off-diagonal cells of the dissociation are populated, which is the sharpest form of "the consumer determines the code" the course has. It also has teeth methodologically: it tells you which endpoint measures which variable.

<strong>Within-modality controls beat between-modality ones</strong> (§8.4, §8.6). Cutaneous against visceral holds the loss function, transduction and first circuit fixed and varies only spatial grain. §5.11's verdict on gustation as a control condition applies here too — a control licenses the negative claim it was built for — but with far fewer confounds to concede.
