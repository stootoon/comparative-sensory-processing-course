title: Final exam
lede: Eleven cumulative questions. No question here is answerable from a single module — each requires you to hold two or more modalities against each other, which is the only skill the course was ever trying to teach. The free-response questions are the centrepiece, and they reward saying what is uncertain.
estimatedMinutes: 55
---

This exam is different in kind from the module quizzes. Those tested whether you could use a module. This tests whether you can use the *comparison* — which means every question is designed so that a reader who mastered one module and skimmed the rest cannot answer it.

Three things earn credit throughout, and they are worth stating in advance because they are not what most exams reward.

**Distinguishing an established result from this course's proposal.** Several claims here are the author's, marked as such in the text and unmarked in most people's memory. Saying which is which is part of the answer.

**Saying what would falsify a claim.** A prediction with no failure condition is a description. Several rubrics award nothing for a correct claim stated unfalsifiably.

**Noticing where the course's own argument is weak.** Several rubrics carry a bonus line for this, and they are not decorative — the free-response model answers do it too.

There is a fourth thing, and it is unusual enough to state separately: **saying that the evidence does not settle a question is a full-credit answer where it is true.** The exam is not testing whether you absorbed a house position on why olfaction lacks a normative theory. There is no house position — §0.0 put four candidate explanations on the table and the course leaves them there. What is being tested is whether you can say what each candidate rests on, what would separate them, and where the eliminations have narrowed the field.

Answer before revealing.

The course opened with a wager: that the clues to understanding olfaction are hidden in how the other modalities work.

The wager paid, and not in the way the framing promised. What the comparison produced was not a set of mechanisms imported into olfaction. That is a more modest product than a theory of smell, and a more durable one. A well-chosen control tells you what a property is not sufficient for, and that outlives every mechanism proposed on top of it.
